Research

Research is of decisive importance when it comes to ensuring that Norway is a knowledge-based and innovative society. Culture, business and industry and welfare all depend on development and use of research.

Research challenges established perceptions and is necessary if we are to be able to take a stance on new phenomena. Furthermore, research is a pre-requisite for an open and critical social debate and helps us understand ourselves and our surroundings better. New knowledge is a spur to new value creation and innovation. Research strengthens the competitive power of business and industry and is a source of economic growth. Research-based knowledge is becoming increasingly important for solving the challenges that the welfare society is facing in areas such as health, working life and the environment.

Research Barometer 2013

International Cooperation in Norwegian Research

[Report, Ministry of Education and Research, 03.06.2013]

The Research Barometer (Forskningsbarometeret) is a publication of the Norwegian Ministry of Education and Research, and has been published yearly since 2011. The report, which also has an accompanying website, presents the latest figures on a broad set of indicators assessing research and innovation, and gives an international benchmarking of Norway’s performance in these areas.
