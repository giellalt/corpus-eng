Outdoor life 

The County Administrative Board is committed to the provision of a rich outdoor life in the county and that the surrounding countryside is accessible to all.

People have different needs and circumstances, and for everyone to have access to nature, we work with both knowledge sharing and making the surroundings available to as many as possible. Some of the natural areas we manage are designed to facilitate access by wheelchair or with prams, for example, there are ramps and disabled parking areas. You are never far from the countryside.

The Right of public access is unique

The general rule of the right of public access is "Do not disturb - do not destroy". The right of public access even applies to nature reserves, but may be limited by various regulations.  

Find out more about the right of public access by visiting the website of the Swedish Environmental Protection Agency.
